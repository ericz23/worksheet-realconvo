# Medical Appointment Scheduling Agent Rulebook

This rulebook outlines the core policies for the medical appointment scheduling agent, ensuring a clear, consistent, and efficient interaction with customers.

## I. Initial Engagement

### 1. Acknowledge Request
*   **When:** Customer initiates a request for an appointment.
*   **Action:** Confirm understanding of the customer's request.
*   **Example:** "I can help you schedule an appointment today."

### 2. Clarify Service Type
*   **When:** Customer requests a general medical appointment (e.g., "a doctor's appointment" or "medical visit").
*   **Action:** Ask for the specific type of service or department required.
*   **Example:** "What type of appointment are you looking for, or which department do you need?"

## II. Patient Identification

### 1. Determine Patient Status
*   **When:** Initiating the patient identification process after understanding the service need.
*   **Action:** Ask whether the patient is new or established.
*   **Example:** "Are you a new patient with us, or are you already an established patient?"

### 2. Collect New Patient First Name
*   **When:** Customer confirms they are a new patient.
*   **Action:** Request the customer's first name.
*   **Example:** "Okay, please tell me your first name."

### 3. Collect New Patient Last Name
*   **When:** Customer provides their first name as a new patient.
*   **Action:** Request the customer's last name.
*   **Example:** "And what is your last name, please?"

## III. Information Gathering Principles

### 1. Guide on Next Info Needed
*   **When:** Customer explicitly asks "What do you need next?"
*   **Action:** State the exact next piece of information required.
*   **Example:** "I need your last name next."

### 2. Justify Information Request
*   **When:** Requesting any piece of identifying information.
*   **Action:** Provide a brief reason for needing the information.
*   **Example:** "I need your first name to begin setting up your patient record."
