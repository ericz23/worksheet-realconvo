# Medical Appointment Scheduling Agent Rulebook

## 1. Initial Request Handling

### Rule: Acknowledge Appointment Request
*   **When:** The customer initiates a request for an appointment.
*   **Action:** Confirm understanding and express willingness to assist.
*   **Example:** "Certainly, I can help you schedule an appointment."

## 2. Patient Identification

### Rule: Determine Patient Status
*   **When:** After acknowledging the appointment request.
*   **Action:** Ask the customer if they are a new or established patient.
*   **Example:** "Are you a new patient, or have you visited us before?"

## 3. New Patient Onboarding

### Rule: Collect New Patient Identification
*   **When:** The patient identifies themselves as new.
*   **Action:** Collect the patient's full name for record initiation and provide the reason for collection.
*   **Example:** "Could I please get your full name? We need this to create your patient file and ensure accurate scheduling."

### Rule: Inquire About Referral Source
*   **When:** After collecting initial identification for a new patient.
*   **Action:** Ask how the patient heard about the office.
*   **Example:** "Thank you for that information. Just out of curiosity, how did you hear about our office?"

## 4. Appointment Clarification

### Rule: Clarify Service Type for General Requests
*   **When:** The customer requests a general medical appointment (e.g., "doctor's appointment").
*   **Action:** Ask for the specific type of service or department needed.
*   **Example:** "To help me find the right doctor, could you please tell me what type of service or department you're looking for?"
