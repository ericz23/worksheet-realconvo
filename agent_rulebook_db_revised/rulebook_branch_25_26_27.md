# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Contact

### Initial Greeting and Assistance
**When:** Call initiated by patient or customer
**Action:** Greet the caller, identify the organization/clinic, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling [Clinic Name], this is [Agent Name]. How may I help you today?

### Clarifying Customer Information
**When:** Customer provides unclear information, expresses confusion, asks for repetition, or requests clarification
**Action:** Clarify or confirm information, repeat or rephrase questions to ensure understanding.
**Example:** I apologize, let me rephrase that. I was asking, are you a new patient with our clinic?

## Patient & Appointment Details

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** After initial greeting, when patient requests to schedule an appointment
**Action:** Inquire if the patient is new or an existing patient.
**Example:** Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited us before?

### Determine Appointment Scope
**When:** After confirming patient status or intent to schedule
**Action:** Ask if the appointment is for the caller only or also for family members.
**Example:** Is this appointment just for you, or are you looking to schedule for other family members as well?

### Gather Family Member Details
**When:** Customer indicates appointment is for a family member
**Action:** Inquire about the family member's patient status (new or established) and, if a child, their age.
**Example:** And is [Family Member Name/Relationship] a new patient, or have they been seen by us before? If a child, could you please tell me their age?

### Ascertain Visit Reason
**When:** After confirming patient status and appointment scope
**Action:** Ask for the primary reason for the visit.
**Example:** What is the primary reason for your visit today?

## Insurance Management

### Inquire About Insurance Use
**When:** After patient identification and initial appointment details (e.g., new patient, appointment scope) are confirmed
**Action:** Inquire if dental insurance will be used for the appointment, specifying for whom if applicable.
**Example:** Will you be using dental insurance for this appointment? And for [Patient's Name], will they be using dental insurance?

### Collect Insurance Provider and Plan Type
**When:** Patient indicates having dental insurance
**Action:** Ask for the dental insurance company's name and the plan type (e.g., PPO, HMO).
**Example:** Could you please tell me your insurance company's name and the type of plan you have, like PPO or HMO?

### Verify In-Network Status
**When:** Valid insurance details (provider and plan type) are provided
**Action:** Check if the office is in-network with the provided insurance plan.
**Example:** Let me quickly check if we are in-network with your [Insurance Company Name] [Plan Type] plan.

## Scheduling Process

### Inquire About Preferred Scheduling Availability
**When:** After patient, insurance (if applicable), and appointment scope details confirmed
**Action:** Inquire about the customer's preferred scheduling availability.
**Example:** Thank you for all that information. What days or times work best for you to come in for your appointment?

### Ascertain Preferred Appointment Month
**When:** After the patient provides general or broad availability (e.g., 'any weekday morning')
**Action:** Ask the patient to specify a preferred month for the appointment.
**Example:** Okay, and do you have a specific month in mind for this appointment?

### Summarize Appointment Preferences
**When:** After all patient, insurance (if applicable), visit reason, and desired availability details have been collected, and before offering specific appointment slots
**Action:** Summarize all confirmed appointment preferences to the patient for verification.
**Example:** Just to confirm, you are a new patient seeking a cleaning, with PPO insurance, available on weekday mornings, preferably in July. Is that correct?

## General Communication

### Acknowledge Customer Input
**When:** Customer makes a statement or provides information
**Action:** Acknowledge understanding with phrases like 'Okay', 'I understand', or similar.
**Example:** Okay, I understand.
