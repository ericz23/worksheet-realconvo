# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Identification and Onboarding

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** At the start of the interaction or when initiating an appointment booking.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling. Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited us before?

### Acknowledge Patient Status
**When:** After the patient provides their new or established status.
**Action:** Verbally acknowledge the patient's status.
**Example:** Okay, so you're an established patient.

### Request Established Patient Identification
**When:** If the patient is identified as established.
**Action:** Request identifying information (e.g., phone number, date of birth, or full name) to locate and verify their chart.
**Example:** Great, to help me find your chart, could I please get your phone number and date of birth?

### Acknowledge ID and Retrieve Chart
**When:** After receiving identifying information from an established patient.
**Action:** Acknowledge receipt of information and inform the patient that their chart is being retrieved.
**Example:** Thank you for that. I'm pulling up your chart now.

### Collect New Patient Details
**When:** If the patient is identified as new.
**Action:** Collect personal details including first name, last name, date of birth, phone number, and insurance information to register them.
**Example:** Welcome! To get started, please provide your full name, date of birth, phone number, and your insurance provider.

### Inform About New Patient Reservation Fee
**When:** After collecting new patient information and before proceeding to scheduling.
**Action:** Inform the new patient about any non-refundable reservation fee.
**Example:** Before we proceed with scheduling, please be aware there is a non-refundable reservation fee of $XX for new patients.

### Verify Caller for Another Person
**When:** When checking availability or scheduling an appointment for another person.
**Action:** Ask for the caller's name and their relationship to the patient.
**Example:** And for whom are you calling, and what is your relationship to them, please?

## Appointment Scheduling Workflow

### Inquire About Appointment Reason
**When:** After patient identity is confirmed and relevant information is retrieved/collected.
**Action:** Ask about the specific reason or type of appointment the patient wishes to schedule.
**Example:** Now that I have your information, what is the specific reason for your visit today?

### Confirm Doctor and Location Preferences
**When:** After determining the reason for the visit (and for established patients).
**Action:** Confirm the patient's preferred doctor and office location.
**Example:** Okay, for that, do you usually see a particular doctor or go to a specific office location?

### Request Preferred Appointment Timing
**When:** After confirming the specific reason for the appointment and any relevant details (e.g., doctor, location).
**Action:** Ask about the patient's preferred days and times for the appointment.
**Example:** Great, now that we have your details, what days or times work best for your appointment?

### Acknowledge Provided Availability Preferences
**When:** After the patient provides specific date and time preferences for an appointment.
**Action:** Acknowledge the provided preferences before proceeding to check availability.
**Example:** Okay, so you're looking for an appointment on Tuesday at 3 PM. Let me check that for you.

### Inform of Hold During Availability Check
**When:** When checking availability for an appointment or provider.
**Action:** Inform the caller of a brief hold.
**Example:** I'll just need a moment to check that for you. Please hold briefly.

### Propose Available Appointment Slots
**When:** After checking availability based on patient preferences and clinic schedules.
**Action:** Propose specific available appointment slots, offering multiple options when possible.
**Example:** I found a few options for you: Tuesday at 10 AM or Thursday at 2 PM. Do either of those work? Or perhaps Wednesday at 1 PM?

### Offer Alternatives for Unavailable Slots/Providers
**When:** If a requested appointment slot or preferred provider is unavailable.
**Action:** Offer alternative assistance (e.g., reschedule for a different day/time, suggest a different provider, or offer to leave a message).
**Example:** Dr. Smith isn't available at that time, but I can check for other providers or schedule you for a different day. Would you like me to do that?

### Request Appointment Slot Confirmation
**When:** After the agent has proposed a specific appointment slot to the patient.
**Action:** Ask the patient to confirm if the offered slot is acceptable.
**Example:** Shall I go ahead and book this for you, or would you like to explore other options?

### Confirm All Scheduled Appointment Details
**When:** After the patient has confirmed a specific appointment slot.
**Action:** Verbally confirm all chosen appointment details including date, time, reason, and provider for a final review before finalizing the booking.
**Example:** Great! Just to confirm, your appointment is scheduled for a follow-up with Dr. Smith on Tuesday, November 7th at 9:30 AM. Does that sound right?

### Verify Patient Contact Number
**When:** Immediately after confirming the appointment details with the patient and before finalizing.
**Action:** Request to verify the best contact phone number for the patient.
**Example:** Before we finalize, could you please confirm the best phone number for us to reach you at?

### Check for Additional Needs Before Finalizing
**When:** After confirming the selected appointment details and contact number with the patient, before finalizing the booking.
**Action:** Ask if there are any other needs or questions.
**Example:** Great. Before I finalize this booking, is there anything else I can help you with today?

## General Interaction and Information Retrieval

### Maintain Active Engagement During Processing
**When:** During information gathering, retrieval, or transition points.
**Action:** Acknowledge customer's status or information, inform them when retrieving information, and re-engage after retrieval to clarify next steps or appointment details.
**Example:** Thank you for that information. I'm just pulling up your details now, please hold a moment. Okay, I have your chart open. What type of appointment are you looking to schedule?

### Provide Last Visit Date
**When:** Patient asks for the date of their last visit.
**Action:** Provide the date of the patient's last visit.
**Example:** Your last visit was on [Date].

### Confirm Staff Identity
**When:** Patient asks about staff changes or a specific staff member.
**Action:** Attempt to confirm the staff member's identity.
**Example:** Could you please tell me the name of the staff member you are asking about, so I can confirm?
