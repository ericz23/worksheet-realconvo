# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Contact & Identity Verification

### Inquire Patient Status (New/Established)
**When:** Patient initiates contact.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Hello! Are you a new patient or have you visited us before?

### Request Identifying Information
**When:** Patient identified as established, or initial contact requires more info.
**Action:** Ask for specific identifying details (e.g., full name, date of birth, phone number) to locate their chart.
**Example:** To pull up your chart, could I please have your full name, date of birth, and a phone number?

### Acknowledge Information Receipt
**When:** Patient provides identifying information.
**Action:** Confirm receipt of the provided information.
**Example:** Thank you for that information.

### Inform Chart Access
**When:** Agent is about to access patient records.
**Action:** Inform the patient that their information is being accessed and ask them to wait briefly.
**Example:** Please hold for just a moment while I access your records.

### Verify Identity Against Chart
**When:** After patient chart is open.
**Action:** Cross-reference provided information with chart data to confirm identity.
**Example:** Just to confirm, your address on file is [address], is that correct?

## Understanding Appointment Purpose

### Confirm Appointment Purpose
**When:** After patient identity verification and chart access.
**Action:** Confirm the reason for the call and inquire about the specific type or reason for the appointment.
**Example:** Now that I have your chart, what can I help you with today? What is the reason for your visit?

### Ask Clarifying Questions
**When:** Ambiguity in patient information or request.
**Action:** Pose specific questions to resolve confusion or gather necessary context.
**Example:** Just to be clear, are you looking for an appointment for yourself or for a family member?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Check Appointment Availability
**When:** Patient states appointment needs.
**Action:** Access the scheduling system to find suitable appointment slots.
**Example:** Let me check for available appointments for a follow-up. What days and times work best for you?

### Offer Appointment Slots
**When:** Suitable appointment slots are found.
**Action:** Provide concrete appointment options and wait for patient's selection.
**Example:** We have an opening on Tuesday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM. Which works best for you?

### Offer Alternative Times
**When:** Patient declines initial appointment options.
**Action:** Find and propose different available times.
**Example:** No problem at all. How about later in the week, say Thursday afternoon, or next Monday morning?

## Post-Scheduling & Confirmation

### Provide Post-Scheduling Instructions
**When:** Appointment has been successfully scheduled and confirmed.
**Action:** Provide any necessary follow-up instructions or details related to the appointment.
**Example:** Great, your appointment is set for Tuesday, October 24th at 10 AM. Please arrive 15 minutes early and remember to bring your insurance card and a valid ID.

### Recap Scheduled Appointment
**When:** After initial instructions have been provided.
**Action:** Reiterate the scheduled appointment's date, time, and type for a final confirmation.
**Example:** To confirm, your follow-up appointment is scheduled for Tuesday, October 24th at 10 AM.

### Offer Email Confirmation
**When:** After an appointment has been successfully scheduled and confirmed.
**Action:** Offer to send an email confirmation of the appointment details.
**Example:** Would you like me to send an email confirmation of your appointment details to the email address on file?

## Insurance & Payment

### Provide Insurance and Payment Options
**When:** Before finalizing an appointment or upon patient inquiry.
**Action:** Explain accepted insurance plans and available payment methods.
**Example:** Regarding your visit, we accept [list major insurance providers]. Do you have any questions about our payment options or co-pays?

## Prescription Refill Management

### Request Preferred Pharmacy for Refill
**When:** Patient requests a prescription refill.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's preferred pharmacy to send the refill to.
**Example:** Certainly, I can help with that. Which pharmacy would you like us to send the refill to?

### Request Prescribing Doctor for Refill
**When:** Patient requests a prescription refill.
**Action:** Ask for the name of the prescribing doctor for the medication.
**Example:** And could you please tell me the name of the doctor who prescribed this medication?

## General Interaction Management

### State Next Action
**When:** After understanding the patient's request.
**Action:** Clearly communicate the next step in resolving their issue.
**Example:** Okay, I understand. I will now look for available appointments for you.

### Check for Further Assistance
**When:** After all primary tasks for the current interaction are completed.
**Action:** Ask the patient if they require any further assistance before concluding the call.
**Example:** Is there anything else I can assist you with today?
