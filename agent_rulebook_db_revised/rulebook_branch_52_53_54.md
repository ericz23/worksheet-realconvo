# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Identification & Verification

### Verify Patient Status
**When:** Upon patient initiating contact for an appointment or inquiry.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or an established patient.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling. Are you a new patient or an established patient with us?

### Acknowledge Patient Status
**When:** Patient confirms their new or established status.
**Action:** Verbally acknowledge the patient's status.
**Example:** Okay, thank you for letting me know you're an established patient.

### Collect Identifying Information
**When:** After acknowledging patient status.
**Action:** Request necessary personal details (e.g., name, date of birth, phone number) to locate or create a patient record.
**Example:** To help me pull up your information, could I please get your full name and date of birth?

### Acknowledge Information Receipt
**When:** Patient provides identifying information.
**Action:** Confirm receipt of the information.
**Example:** Got it, thank you.

### Inform Chart Retrieval
**When:** After collecting identifying information for an established patient.
**Action:** Inform the patient that their chart is being retrieved and ask for a moment of patience.
**Example:** Please bear with me for just a moment while I pull up your chart.

### Confirm Existing Provider/Location
**When:** An established patient's chart has been successfully located.
**Action:** Confirm the patient's usual provider and preferred location from their chart.
**Example:** And just to confirm, you usually see Dr. Smith at our downtown clinic, correct?

### Verify Chart Details
**When:** An established patient's chart is open and the desired appointment type has been clarified.
**Action:** Confirm key patient details (e.g., address, insurance information) from the chart with the patient.
**Example:** Before we schedule, can I confirm your current address on file is 123 Main St.?

## Appointment Details

### Determine Appointment Reason
**When:** After patient identification/verification.
**Action:** Inquire about the purpose of the appointment request and details of the concern.
**Example:** What is the reason for your visit today? Or could you tell me a little more about what's bringing you in?

### Clarify Appointment Type
**When:** After determining the appointment reason.
**Action:** Ask what type of appointment the patient is seeking.
**Example:** What type of appointment are you looking to schedule today? Is it for a general check-up, a follow-up, or something else?

## Insurance Information

### Inquire About Insurance
**When:** After determining the appointment reason.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's insurance carrier details.
**Example:** Do you have insurance, and if so, what is your provider?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Inquire General Availability
**When:** After clarifying appointment type, preferred provider, and location.
**Action:** Ask the patient about their general availability preferences (e.g., preferred days, times, date ranges).
**Example:** To help me find the best time for you, what days or times generally work best for your schedule for this check-up?

### Search for Appointment Slots
**When:** Patient provides specific desired days and/or times for an appointment with a confirmed provider and location.
**Action:** Access the scheduling system to search for available appointment slots that match the patient's specific requests.
**Example:** Okay, you're looking for Dr. Smith on a Tuesday afternoon at the downtown clinic. Let me check what's available.

### Offer Appointment Slots
**When:** After searching for available appointment slots based on patient preferences.
**Action:** Present specific available date and time options to the patient.
**Example:** Okay, Dr. Sharma has openings on November 10th at 10 AM or 2 PM. Which time works best for you?

### Confirm Selected Appointment
**When:** After the patient selects a preferred appointment slot.
**Action:** Verbally confirm the chosen doctor, date, and time with the patient.
**Example:** Just to confirm, you'd like to schedule an appointment with Dr. Sharma on November 10th at 2 PM. Is that correct?

### Offer Post-Confirmation Assistance
**When:** After an appointment has been successfully confirmed.
**Action:** Proactively ask the patient if there is anything else they need assistance with.
**Example:** Your appointment is now confirmed. Is there anything else I can help you with today?
