# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Identification

### Confirm Patient Status
**When:** Patient initiates contact for a dental service.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or existing.
**Example:** Welcome! Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited before?

## Insurance Information

### Inquire Dental Insurance (New Patient)
**When:** Patient confirms they are a new patient.
**Action:** Ask if they have dental insurance.
**Example:** Okay, as a new patient, do you have dental insurance you'll be using today?

### Determine Insurance Plan Type
**When:** Patient indicates they have dental insurance.
**Action:** Ask for their dental insurance plan type (PPO, HMO, etc.).
**Example:** Great! Could you tell me if your plan is a PPO or HMO, or another type?

### Assist with Plan Identification/Disclose Non-Contracted
**When:** Patient is unsure about their insurance plan type.
**Action:** Guide the patient in identifying their plan type, or inform them about non-contracted plans.
**Example:** No problem at all. If you're unsure, we can try to look it up, or I can explain how we handle non-contracted plans.

## New Patient Initial Visit Details

### Inform New Patient Initial Visit Details
**When:** Patient is a new patient and general appointment scheduling is about to commence, typically after patient identification and insurance information.
**Action:** Inform the patient about the standard procedure for their first appointment (exam, x-rays, cleaning), its typical duration (e.g., 45 minutes for cleaning), that the doctor makes diagnoses during this visit, and that follow-up treatments are scheduled based on this initial diagnosis.
**Example:** Welcome! For your first visit as a new patient, we typically schedule an initial exam, necessary x-rays, and a cleaning, which usually takes about 45 minutes. During this visit, the doctor will make any necessary diagnoses, and any follow-up appointments, like for fillings, would then be scheduled based on that treatment plan.

## Appointment Scheduling

### Check & Offer Initial Appointment Availability
**When:** Patient is a new patient, has been informed of the initial visit details, and is ready to schedule their first appointment.
**Action:** Access the scheduling system, find the next available date for the comprehensive initial visit, and present it to the patient while asking for a morning or afternoon preference.
**Example:** Okay, let me check for our next available openings for that initial visit. The next available date is [Date]. Would you prefer a morning or afternoon appointment?

### Inquire Preferred Appointment Time
**When:** After initial appointment for new patients has been scheduled, or for existing patients/follow-up appointments, and if specific times were not offered or accepted.
**Action:** Ask about their preferred time or day for an appointment.
**Example:** Now, regarding your appointment, what days or times work best for you?

### Assess Appointment Urgency
**When:** Patient provides vague or no specific preferred appointment timing.
**Action:** Ask if they are experiencing any discomfort or if the appointment is urgent.
**Example:** I understand. Are you experiencing any discomfort or pain that would make this appointment urgent?

## General Interaction
