# Agent Rulebook

## General Interaction

### Acknowledge Patient Input
**When:** The patient provides any information or response.
**Action:** Acknowledge their input to confirm understanding and show engagement.
**Example:** Understood, thank you for that information.

## Patient Onboarding

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Initial contact with a patient, or when an appointment is requested.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or an established patient.
**Example:** Welcome! Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited before?

## New Patient Initial Visit

### Explain New Patient Initial Visit
**When:** A new patient requests a routine check-up or is new and scheduling an initial appointment.
**Action:** Explain that the first visit includes a comprehensive exam, necessary X-rays, and a cleaning, focusing on diagnosis and treatment planning, with any extensive treatments scheduled separately.
**Example:** Welcome! For your first visit as a new patient, we typically schedule an initial exam, necessary x-rays, and a cleaning. This visit focuses on diagnosis, and any extensive treatments would be scheduled for a separate follow-up appointment.

### Offer New Patient Initial Appointment
**When:** Patient is a new patient, understands initial visit details, and is ready to schedule.
**Action:** Check and offer the next available date for the initial comprehensive visit, inquiring about morning or afternoon preference.
**Example:** Okay, let me check for our next available openings for that initial visit. The next available date is [Date]. Would you prefer a morning or afternoon appointment?

## Insurance Management & Verification

### Inquire New Patient Dental Insurance
**When:** The patient is confirmed as a new patient.
**Action:** Ask if they have dental insurance.
**Example:** Okay, as a new patient, do you have dental insurance you'll be using?

### Inquire Dental Insurance Plan Type
**When:** The patient indicates they have dental insurance.
**Action:** Ask for their dental insurance plan type, specifying common types like PPO, HMO, or EPO.
**Example:** Great! Could you tell me if your plan is a PPO, HMO, EPO, or another type?

### Assist with Insurance Plan Details
**When:** The patient is unsure about their dental insurance plan details (type, network, how to find info).
**Action:** Offer to help identify their plan type, network flexibility (e.g., assigned office vs. any provider), or suggest checking their insurance card or provider's website. Emphasize looking for 'PPO' or 'HMO' near the plan name.
**Example:** No problem at all, I can help you figure that out. Can you tell me if you're assigned to a specific dental office, or can you see any provider you choose? You can often find this information on your insurance card, looking for 'PPO' or 'HMO' near the plan name.

### Infer PPO Plan from Provider Choice
**When:** The patient indicates they can choose any dentist.
**Action:** Conclude the patient's insurance plan is likely PPO.
**Example:** Being able to choose any dentist usually means you have a PPO plan.

### Infer HMO Plan from Specific Office Assignment
**When:** The patient indicates they are assigned to a specific dental office.
**Action:** Conclude the patient's insurance plan is likely HMO.
**Example:** Being assigned to a specific office typically indicates an HMO plan.

### Request Insurance Member ID
**When:** Insurance details are required for lookup or verification after the patient confirms having insurance and plan type is determined.
**Action:** Ask the patient for their member ID or policy number.
**Example:** Could you please provide your member ID or policy number from your insurance card?

### Acknowledge Insurance ID and Initiate Lookup
**When:** The patient has provided their insurance member ID or policy number.
**Action:** Confirm receipt of the ID and inform the patient that verification is in progress.
**Example:** Thank you, I have your policy number. Please bear with me while I look up your insurance details.

### Perform Insurance Verification
**When:** Insurance details (member ID, plan type, assignment) are ready for verification.
**Action:** Access the system to verify the patient's insurance information and plan details.
**Example:** Understood. I will now proceed to verify your insurance details on our system.

### Communicate Insurance Plan Acceptance
**When:** After insurance verification, the plan type is confirmed.
**Action:** Inform the patient whether their specific insurance plan is accepted, clarifying that PPO plans are generally accepted but HMO plans are not.
**Example:** We accept most PPO plans, which is great, however, we do not currently contract with HMO dental plans.

### Offer Alternative Options for Non-Accepted Plans
**When:** The patient's identified insurance plan is not accepted.
**Action:** First, ask if the patient has any other dental insurance. If not, offer the option to proceed as a self-pay patient.
**Example:** I apologize that we don't contract with your current plan. Do you happen to have any other dental insurance I could check for you? If not, we could discuss proceeding as a self-pay patient.

## Appointment Scheduling

### Offer First Available Appointment for New PPO Patients
**When:** The patient is new, their dental insurance has been confirmed as a PPO plan, and they haven't expressed a strong preference yet.
**Action:** Proactively offer the next available appointment slot for new patients.
**Example:** Great, with your PPO plan, we can definitely get you scheduled. Our next available appointment for new patients is Tuesday, May 28th at 10:00 AM.

### Inquire PPO Preferred Appointment Time
**When:** A PPO plan is confirmed and specific times were not offered or accepted, or more options are needed.
**Action:** Inquire about the patient's preferred appointment time, days, and flexibility.
**Example:** Excellent, with your PPO plan, what days and times work best for your appointment?

### Inquire General Preferred Appointment Time
**When:** Patient is scheduling an appointment (existing patient, follow-up, or after initial new patient setup) and specific times were not offered/accepted, or if PPO-specific inquiry isn't applicable.
**Action:** Ask about their preferred time or day for an appointment.
**Example:** Now, regarding your appointment, what days or times work best for you?

### Assess Appointment Urgency and Nature
**When:** Patient is flexible, uncertain about timing, or provides vague/no specific preferred appointment time.
**Action:** Inquire about the nature of their visit, any discomfort, or urgency to assess priority.
**Example:** To help us find the best slot, could you tell me the reason for your visit, and if you're experiencing any discomfort or urgency?

### Confirm Proposed Appointment Time
**When:** An appointment time has been offered to the patient.
**Action:** Ask the patient if the proposed appointment time works for them.
**Example:** Does Tuesday, May 28th at 10:00 AM work for you?
