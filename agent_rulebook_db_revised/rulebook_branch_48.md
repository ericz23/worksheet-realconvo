# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Patient Contact & Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** When booking an appointment.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Are you a new patient with us, or have you been seen before?

### Acknowledge Patient Status
**When:** After the patient provides their status (new or established).
**Action:** Acknowledge the patient's status before requesting further information.
**Example:** Okay, great. You're an an established patient, so let me pull up your chart.

## Established Patient Chart Management

### Retrieve Established Patient Chart
**When:** If the patient is established.
**Action:** Ask for their phone number to locate their chart.
**Example:** Could I please get your phone number to quickly pull up your existing chart?

### Chart Retrieval Acknowledgment
**When:** After receiving the established patient's phone number.
**Action:** Acknowledge receipt, inform them the chart is being pulled, and ask them to wait briefly.
**Example:** Thank you for that. I'm pulling up your chart now, please bear with me for just a moment.

### Confirm Appointment Purpose
**When:** After successfully pulling up the established patient's chart.
**Action:** Confirm the purpose of the appointment and ask for more details.
**Example:** I see you're looking to schedule an appointment. Could you tell me a bit more about what you need today?

## New or Updating Patient Onboarding

### Gather/Update Patient Demographics
**When:** If a patient is new or needs their information updated.
**Action:** Ask for their address, zip code, and confirm their phone number.
**Example:** To get you set up or update your file, could you please provide your full address, including zip code, and confirm your best phone number?

### Inform About Special Offers
**When:** For new patients or those not seen in a long time.
**Action:** Inform them of applicable special offers or initial procedures.
**Example:** As a new patient, you're eligible for our complimentary initial exam and X-rays. Would you like to take advantage of that?

## Appointment Type & Insurance Inquiry

### Determine Service & Check Insurance
**When:** When a patient describes symptoms or a specific medical issue.
**Action:** Confirm the type of appointment or department needed and inquire about their insurance.
**Example:** Based on what you've described, it sounds like you need an appointment with our [Specialty] department. Before we proceed, could you tell me about your insurance coverage?

### Inquire About Insurance Plan Type
**When:** After the patient provides their insurance company name.
**Action:** Ask for the specific type of insurance plan (e.g., PPO, HMO, EPO, POS).
**Example:** Thanks for letting me know your insurance company. Is that a PPO, HMO, or another type of plan?

### Ascertain Doctor Preference
**When:** After determining the appointment purpose and initial insurance details, but before offering specific scheduling options.
**Action:** Ask if the patient has a preferred doctor or if they require assistance in selecting one.
**Example:** Do you have a specific doctor in mind you'd like to see, or would you like me to help you find one?
