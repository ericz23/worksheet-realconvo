# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Status & Initial Inquiry

### Confirm Patient Status
**When:** Patient is booking an appointment.
**Action:** Confirm if the patient is new or existing.
**Example:** Are you a new or existing patient with us?

## Insurance Verification

### Inquire Dental Insurance (New Patients)
**When:** Patient is new.
**Action:** Ask about dental insurance.
**Example:** Do you have dental insurance?

### Identify Insurance Plan Type
**When:** Patient has indicated dental insurance.
**Action:** Ask for the plan type (PPO/HMO).
**Example:** Is your plan PPO or HMO?

### Assist with Plan Type Identification
**When:** Patient is unsure about the plan type (PPO/HMO).
**Action:** Attempt to help identify the plan or state non-contracted plans.
**Example:** No worries, we can help check for you. Which insurance provider do you have? Please note we might not be contracted with all HMO plans.

## Appointment Scheduling

### Request Preferred Appointment Time (PPO)
**When:** A PPO plan is confirmed.
**Action:** Inquire about the patient's preferred appointment time.
**Example:** Great, what day and time works best for your appointment?

### Determine Appointment Urgency/Nature
**When:** No preferred appointment time is specified OR customer asks about general availability.
**Action:** Inquire about the nature of their visit, discomfort, or urgency.
**Example:** To help us find the best slot, could you tell me the reason for your visit, and if you're experiencing any discomfort or urgency?

### Explain New Patient Routine Visit Procedure
**When:** A new patient requests a routine check-up and confirms no immediate concerns.
**Action:** Explain the typical procedure for the initial appointment, including services provided (e.g., exam, X-rays, cleaning) and the process for subsequent appointments based on diagnosis.
**Example:** For your first visit, as a new patient, we typically start with a comprehensive exam, necessary X-rays, and a professional cleaning. Afterwards, the dentist will discuss any findings and recommend next steps for any future treatments.

## General Interaction

### Acknowledge Customer Responses
**When:** Customer provides a response.
**Action:** Acknowledge the customer's response.
**Example:** Understood, thank you for letting me know.
