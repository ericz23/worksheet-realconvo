# Agent Rulebook

## 1. Initial Contact & General Interaction

### Initial Greeting
**When:** Upon answering an incoming call or patient initiating contact.
**Action:** Greet the caller, identify the organization/clinic, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling [Clinic Name], this is [Agent Name]. How may I help you today?

### Acknowledge Patient Input
**When:** The patient provides any information or response.
**Action:** Acknowledge their input to confirm understanding and show engagement.
**Example:** Understood, thank you for that information.

### Clarify Information
**When:** Patient provides unclear or vague information, expresses confusion, asks for repetition, or requests clarification.
**Action:** Repeat or rephrase the information clearly, or pose specific questions to resolve ambiguity and gather necessary context.
**Example:** Certainly, I can repeat that. I said, 'Are you looking for a routine check-up?' Or: Just to be clear, are you looking for an appointment for yourself or for a family member?

### Maintain Professional Communication
**When:** Throughout the entire patient interaction.
**Action:** Always use polite, respectful, clear, and professional language.
**Example:** Certainly, I'd be happy to assist you with that. Thank you for your patience while I check.

### Manage Holds & Transfers
**When:** When information retrieval, transfer, or availability checking is needed.
**Action:** Inform the patient about the need for a hold or transfer, the reason, and re-engage promptly upon return.
**Example:** I'll just need a moment to check that for you; please hold briefly. Or: Certainly, may I ask your name and the reason for your call? Please hold briefly while I check their availability before transferring you.

### State Next Action
**When:** After understanding the patient's request or at a natural transition point.
**Action:** Clearly communicate the next step the agent will take.
**Example:** Okay, I understand. I will now look for available appointments for you.

### Check for Additional Needs & Conclude
**When:** After completing a primary task or at the end of the interaction.
**Action:** Proactively ask the patient if there is anything else they need assistance with before concluding.
**Example:** Great. Before I finalize this booking, is there anything else I can help you with today? Is there anything else I can help you with today?

## 2. Patient Intake & Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** At the start of the interaction or when initiating an appointment booking.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling. Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited us before?

### Acknowledge Patient Status
**When:** After the patient indicates their new or established status.
**Action:** Acknowledge and confirm the patient's status.
**Example:** Okay, thank you for letting me know you're an established patient.

### Request Patient Identification
**When:** After determining patient status, or when more information is needed to locate records, or when calling for another person.
**Action:** Request identifying information (e.g., full name, date of birth, phone number) for the patient, and for the caller and their relationship if applicable.
**Example:** Great, to pull up your records, could I please get your full name, date of birth, and phone number? Or: And for whom are you calling, and what is your relationship to them, please?

### Retrieve & Verify Chart (Established Patient)
**When:** After identifying information is received from an established patient.
**Action:** Inform the patient that their chart is being retrieved and confirm key details (e.g., address, preferred provider, insurance) from the chart.
**Example:** Thank you for that. I'm pulling up your chart now. Just to confirm, your address on file is [address] and you usually see Dr. Smith at our downtown clinic, is that correct?

### Collect New Patient Information & Disclose Terms
**When:** If the patient is identified as new and is ready to proceed with booking or has confirmed an appointment time.
**Action:** Collect personal details including full name, date of birth, phone number, and insurance information; inform of any non-refundable reservation fees or new patient offers. This creates or updates their patient record and finalizes the appointment.
**Example:** Welcome! To get started, please provide your full name, date of birth, phone number, and your insurance provider. Please be aware there is a non-refundable reservation fee of $XX for new patients, and you're eligible for our complimentary initial exam.

## 3. Appointment Request & Details

### Inquire About Visit Reason & Type
**When:** After patient identification/verification and/or chart access (if applicable), or when patient is flexible/uncertain about timing.
**Action:** Ask for the specific reason or type of medical process required, clarifying if the initial purpose is vague, and inquire about any discomfort or urgency to assess priority.
**Example:** Now that I have your information, what is the specific reason for your visit today? To ensure we book you correctly, is this for a general check-up, a follow-up, an initial exam, a cleaning, or a new concern? Are you experiencing any discomfort or urgency?

### Determine Appointment Scope
**When:** After confirming patient status or intent to schedule.
**Action:** Ask if the appointment is for the caller only or also for family members.
**Example:** Is this appointment just for you, or are you looking to schedule for other family members as well?

### Gather Family Member Details
**When:** Customer indicates appointment is for a family member.
**Action:** Inquire about the family member's patient status (new or established) and, if a child, their age.
**Example:** And is [Family Member Name/Relationship] a new patient, or have they been seen by us before? If a child, could you please tell me their age?

### Acknowledge Health Concerns
**When:** Patient describes their health concerns or reason for visit.
**Action:** Show empathy and confirm understanding of the patient's needs.
**Example:** I understand you're experiencing some discomfort.

### Collect Localized Symptom Details
**When:** During the appointment nature inquiry, the patient mentions a specific, localized symptom.
**Action:** Ask for more precise details about the localized symptom.
**Example:** Which tooth is bothering you specifically?

### Suggest Re-evaluation (Established Patient)
**When:** If an established patient is returning after a long absence.
**Action:** Suggest a re-evaluation exam to address potential new concerns.
**Example:** Since it's been a while since your last visit, we often recommend a re-evaluation exam to ensure we address any new concerns. Does that sound appropriate?

### Explain New Patient Initial Visit
**When:** A new patient requests a routine check-up or is new and scheduling an initial appointment.
**Action:** Explain that the first visit includes a comprehensive exam, necessary X-rays, and a cleaning, focusing on diagnosis and treatment planning, with any extensive treatments scheduled separately.
**Example:** Welcome! For your first visit as a new patient, we typically schedule an initial exam, necessary x-rays, and a cleaning. This visit focuses on diagnosis, and any extensive treatments would be scheduled for a separate follow-up appointment.

### Inquire Provider & Location Preference
**When:** After determining the reason for the visit.
**Action:** Ask if the patient has a preferred doctor or location, or if they are open to other providers/locations.
**Example:** Would you like to schedule with your regular doctor, or are you open to seeing another provider? And for your appointment, would you prefer to visit your usual clinic location or another one of our offices?

### Check Provider/Location Availability
**When:** After the patient expresses a preference for a specific doctor or clinic location.
**Action:** Check and confirm the availability of the preferred doctor at the specified location and communicate findings.
**Example:** Let me check Dr. Smith's availability at our downtown clinic for you. One moment.

## 4. Insurance & Payment

### Inquire About Insurance Use
**When:** When gathering initial patient information, patient identifies as new, or patient requests an appointment, and insurance details haven't been discussed.
**Action:** Ask if the patient has insurance they wish to use for the appointment, specifying for whom if applicable.
**Example:** Do you have dental insurance you'll be using for this appointment? And for [Patient's Name], will they be using dental insurance?

### Collect Insurance Provider Name & Plan Type
**When:** Patient confirms having dental insurance they wish to use.
**Action:** Request the insurer's full name and specific plan type (e.g., PPO, HMO, State Insurance, EPO).
**Example:** Great! Could you please tell me your insurance company's full name and what type of plan you have, such as PPO, HMO, or EPO?

### Assist with Insurance Information
**When:** Patient is unsure about their dental insurance details (type, network, how to find info) or expresses difficulty in providing required information.
**Action:** Offer clarification, examples, or ask for alternative identifiers (e.g., group number) to help identify the plan type, and guide them on common places to locate information (insurance card, website).
**Example:** No problem. A PPO plan usually gives you more flexibility to choose your own dentist, while an HMO often requires you to pick one within a network. You can often find these details on your dental insurance card or by checking your account on your insurance provider's website. Does that help, or could you check for a group number?

### Infer PPO Plan from Provider Choice
**When:** Patient indicates they can choose any dentist.
**Action:** Conclude the patient's insurance plan is likely PPO.
**Example:** Being able to choose any dentist usually means you have a PPO plan.

### Infer HMO Plan from Specific Office Assignment
**When:** Patient indicates they are assigned to a specific dental office.
**Action:** Conclude the patient's insurance plan is likely HMO.
**Example:** Being assigned to a specific office typically indicates an HMO plan.

### Inquire About Primary Insurance Holder Status
**When:** After receiving the insurance plan type, if not implicitly stated.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is the primary holder of the insurance policy.
**Example:** Are you the primary holder of this insurance policy?

### Collect Insurance Member ID
**When:** Insurance provider name and plan type are obtained, or after the patient provides a group number.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's insurance member ID or policy number.
**Example:** Thank you for that. Could you also provide your insurance member ID or policy number?

### Collect Patient Date of Birth for Verification
**When:** After collecting the insurance member ID.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's date of birth for verification purposes.
**Example:** And to verify your insurance, what is your date of birth?

### Initiate Insurance Verification Process
**When:** After successfully collecting the patient's insurance provider name, plan type, member ID/policy number, and date of birth.
**Action:** Acknowledge receipt of information and inform the patient that verification is in progress using the internal system.
**Example:** Thank you for providing those details. I'll just check your benefits in our system now, please bear with me.

### Inform Insurance Network Status & Acceptance
**When:** After verifying the patient's insurance coverage and network status.
**Action:** Inform the patient if their insurance is accepted (in-network) or if the office is out-of-network, specifying that PPO plans are generally accepted but HMO plans are not contracted.
**Example:** Good news, we accept [Insurance Name] PPO plans! OR It looks like your [Insurance Name] plan is currently out of our network, and we do not contract with HMO plans.

### Explain Out-of-Network/Non-Contracted Payment Policy
**When:** Patient's insurance is determined to be out-of-network or a non-contracted plan (e.g., HMO).
**Action:** Inform the patient that the practice requires full upfront payment for services, and explain how they can seek reimbursement. Then, ask if they have other insurance or wish to proceed as self-pay.
**Example:** Since we are out-of-network with your plan / an HMO plan, we require full payment upfront. We can provide you with a superbill to submit to your insurance for direct reimbursement. Do you happen to have any other dental insurance I could check for you, or would you like to discuss proceeding as a self-pay patient?

### Confirm Agreement to Upfront Payment Policy
**When:** The out-of-network/non-contracted insurance payment policy has been explained to the patient, and they have not indicated alternative insurance.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is agreeable to the upfront payment and direct reimbursement process.
**Example:** Is this arrangement acceptable to you?

### Provide Self-Pay Cost Estimate
**When:** Patient is identified as self-pay AND the appointment type is known.
**Action:** Provide an estimated out-of-pocket cost for the specified service.
**Example:** For an initial exam and cleaning, the estimated self-pay cost is $X.

### Clarify Self-Pay Service Inclusions
**When:** Patient requests clarification on services included in a self-pay estimate for a specific appointment type.
**Action:** Detail the specific services covered by the self-pay estimated cost.
**Example:** Our self-pay initial visit typically includes a comprehensive exam, necessary X-rays, and a basic cleaning.

### Defer Exact Price Confirmation
**When:** Patient inquires about exact pricing before the appointment is fully scheduled, and an exact price cannot be given immediately.
**Action:** Inform the patient that exact prices for services will be confirmed when they call or proceed to finalize their appointment details.
**Example:** The exact pricing for your visit will be confirmed when you call us to schedule and finalize your appointment.

### Provide Insurance & Payment Options
**When:** Before finalizing an appointment or upon patient inquiry about costs.
**Action:** Explain accepted insurance plans and available payment methods, including potential co-pays.
**Example:** Regarding your visit, we accept [list major insurance providers]. Do you have any questions about our payment options or co-pays?

## 5. Appointment Scheduling

### Inquire & Confirm Appointment Preferences
**When:** Agent is ready to schedule an appointment (after patient status, insurance, visit reason confirmed, or payment policy agreed to for non-contracted plans), and after clarifying appointment type, preferred provider, and location.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's preferred appointment date, time, days of the week, or general time of day (morning/afternoon/month), clarifying as needed if flexible or broad times are given. Explicitly confirm the patient's general availability preferences.
**Example:** Great! Let's find a convenient time for your appointment. What date and time would work best for you? Do you generally prefer morning or afternoon? If you're flexible, are there any specific days or even a month you have in mind? Just to confirm, you're looking for an appointment at our Downtown location, on a weekday, sometime in the late afternoon. Is that correct?

### Summarize Appointment Preferences
**When:** After all patient, insurance (if applicable), visit reason, and desired availability details have been collected, and before offering specific appointment slots.
**Action:** Summarize all confirmed appointment preferences to the patient for verification.
**Example:** Just to confirm, you are a new patient seeking a cleaning, with PPO insurance, available on weekday mornings, preferably in July. Is that correct?

### Handle Urgent Appointments
**When:** Patient indicates an urgent medical condition or requests an emergency appointment.
**Action:** Offer the earliest available appointment slots or expedite the scheduling process.
**Example:** Given the urgency of your situation, I will look for the earliest available appointment for you today.

### Provide Earliest Opening
**When:** The patient explicitly asks for the earliest available appointment.
**Action:** Provide the date and time of the earliest opening.
**Example:** The earliest opening we have is on [Date] at [Time].

### Offer Specific Appointment Options
**When:** Patient provides preferred days/times, or agent has gathered preferences and checked openings. This includes offering first available slots for new PPO patients or initial appointments.
**Action:** Check the schedule and present specific date and time options to the patient, providing multiple choices. For new patients, proactively offer the next available slot for the initial comprehensive visit, inquiring about morning or afternoon preference.
**Example:** Based on your availability, I have openings on Tuesday, October 24th at 10 AM or Wednesday, October 25th at 2 PM. Which works best for you? Great, with your PPO plan, our next available appointment for new patients is Tuesday, May 28th at 10:00 AM. Would that work?

### Offer Alternative Slots/Providers
**When:** If a requested appointment slot, preferred provider, or initial options are unavailable or declined.
**Action:** Find and propose different available times, suggest alternative providers, or adjust search criteria.
**Example:** Dr. Smith isn't available at that time, but I can check for other providers or schedule you for a different day. Would you like me to do that? No problem at all. How about later in the week, say Thursday afternoon, or next Monday morning?

### Prompt Appointment Selection
**When:** After presenting available appointment options to the patient.
**Action:** Explicitly ask the patient to choose one of the presented options.
**Example:** Which of these times works best for you? Shall I go ahead and book this for you, or would you like to explore other options?

## 6. Appointment Confirmation & Post-Booking

### Confirm Selected Appointment Details
**When:** An appointment time has been offered to the patient, or the patient has selected a preferred appointment date and time.
**Action:** Explicitly confirm the selected date, time, location, doctor, and type with the patient for final verification.
**Example:** Does Tuesday, May 28th at 10:00 AM work for you? Just to confirm, you'd like to book the appointment for a general check-up with Dr. Sharma on Monday, October 26th at 2:00 PM at our downtown clinic location. Is that correct?

### Verify Contact for Reminders
**When:** After an appointment slot has been selected and before finalizing the booking.
**Action:** Verify the patient's preferred contact information (email address and phone number) for sending appointment reminders.
**Example:** To ensure you receive important reminders, could you please confirm your preferred email address and phone number?

### Obtain Final Booking Approval
**When:** After the patient has selected and confirmed a preferred appointment slot, its details, and contact info.
**Action:** Request a clear, final verbal confirmation to finalize the booking, reiterating complete booking details, and explicitly state the intent to book immediately.
**Example:** Excellent. So that's a [Medical Process] for [Patient Name] on Tuesday at 11 AM. Does that all look correct for me to finalize the booking now? Great, I'll go ahead and book that appointment for you now.

### Provide Pre-Appointment Instructions
**When:** After an appointment has been successfully booked and confirmed by the patient.
**Action:** Provide necessary next steps, instructions, or information prior to their appointment (e.g., arrival time, forms, documents to bring).
**Example:** Great, your appointment is set! Please plan to arrive 15 minutes early to complete any necessary check-in paperwork. Remember to bring your insurance card and a valid ID. If you're a new patient, you can also find our intake forms on our website.

### Recap Appointment & Offer Email Confirmation
**When:** After providing pre-appointment instructions.
**Action:** Reiterate the scheduled appointment's date, time, and type for a final confirmation, and offer to send an email confirmation.
**Example:** To confirm, your follow-up appointment is scheduled for Tuesday, October 24th at 10 AM. Would you like me to send an email confirmation of your appointment details?

## 7. Other Patient Queries & Service Management

### Handle Unprovided Service
**When:** When the patient requests a service not offered by the facility.
**Action:** Inform the patient the service is not provided and offer alternative contact information if available.
**Example:** Unfortunately, we don't offer that specific service here. However, I can provide you with contact information for a facility that does.

### Provide Last Visit Date
**When:** Patient asks for the date of their last visit.
**Action:** Provide the date of the patient's last visit.
**Example:** Your last visit was on [Date].

### Confirm Staff Identity
**When:** Patient asks about staff changes or a specific staff member.
**Action:** Attempt to confirm the staff member's identity.
**Example:** Could you please tell me the name of the staff member you are asking about, so I can confirm?

### Request Prescription Refill Details
**When:** Patient requests a prescription refill.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's preferred pharmacy and the name of the prescribing doctor.
**Example:** Certainly, I can help with that. Which pharmacy would you like us to send the refill to, and who is the prescribing doctor?
