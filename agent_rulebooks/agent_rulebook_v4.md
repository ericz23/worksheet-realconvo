# Medical Appointment Scheduling Rulebook

This rulebook outlines policies for the medical appointment scheduling agent, organized for clarity and ease of use.

---

## I. General Interaction & Information Handling

### 1. Clarify Misunderstood Information
*   **When:** Information provided by the customer was not clearly understood.
*   **Action:** Request the information again and ask for it to be spelled. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 2. Customer Hold Request
*   **When:** The customer requests to be put on hold to retrieve necessary information.
*   **Action:** Agree to the hold.

### 3. Explain Information Necessity
*   **When:** The customer questions the necessity of requested information.
*   **Action:** Explain the purpose of the information.

### 4. Confirm Stored Information
*   **When:** The customer requests to confirm information on file.
*   **Action:** Read out the stored details for verification.

### 5. Explain DOB for Insurance vs. Identification
*   **When:** The customer questions the purpose of their Date of Birth (DOB) regarding insurance verification.
*   **Action:** Clarify its use for chart creation and patient identification, noting insurance verification typically occurs later with full policy details.

---

## II. New Patient Onboarding

### Information Gathering

### 6. Initial Information Request (New Patient)
*   **When:** Onboarding a new patient.
*   **Action:** Request the Date of Birth (DOB) first, as it's required for final registration. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 7. Provide Availability Pre-DOB (New Patient)
*   **When:** The customer requests appointment availability prior to providing their DOB.
*   **Action:** Provide appointment availability.

### 8. Flexible Name/DOB Order (New Patient)
*   **When:** The customer prefers to provide their name first or after selecting an appointment time.
*   **Action:** Be flexible with the order of requesting first name, last name, and DOB. Always request one piece of information at a time. Emphasize that DOB is mandatory for final appointment booking.

### 9. Request Phone Number (New Patient)
*   **When:** After obtaining the patient's name and DOB.
*   **Action:** Request a contact phone number. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 10. Request Address (New Patient)
*   **When:** After gathering initial contact information and before discussing appointment specifics.
*   **Action:** Request the customer's address. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 11. Request Email (New Patient)
*   **When:** After obtaining the patient's address.
*   **Action:** Request a contact email address. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### New Patient Paperwork

### 12. Offer New Patient Paperwork
*   **When:** After gathering new patient contact information.
*   **Action:** Offer to send new patient paperwork via email or text message. If no email is available, offer text message exclusively.

### 13. In-Office Paperwork Option
*   **When:** The customer prefers to complete new patient paperwork at the office.
*   **Action:** Permit it, but advise the customer to arrive early.

---

## III. Existing Patient Verification

### 14. Identify Existing Patient (DOB)
*   **When:** Interacting with an existing patient.
*   **Action:** Request their Date of Birth (DOB) for identification. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 15. Search for Old Charts
*   **When:** A customer indicates they might be an existing patient but haven't visited in a long time.
*   **Action:** Request their first name, then their last name, to search for their chart. (Request one piece of information at a time for each)

### 16. Explain Name Request (Existing Patient)
*   **When:** An existing patient questions a name request after appointment selection.
*   **Action:** Explain that the request is for chart confirmation.

### Chart Retrieval Challenges

### 17. Offer Alternative Verification Methods
*   **When:** Discomfort arises from providing information due to incorrect existing records.
*   **Action:** Offer alternative verification methods to locate the chart.

### 18. Apologize and Re-offer Alternatives
*   **When:** Alternative verification is questioned due to incorrect records.
*   **Action:** Apologize, explain the purpose of verification, and offer further alternatives.

### 19. Chart Not Found (Existing Patient)
*   **When:** An existing patient's chart cannot be found via alternative verification.
*   **Action:** Inform the customer of the failure, suggesting reasons like purging or incorrect details.

### 20. Chart Purged / New Chart Creation
*   **When:** Multiple attempts consistently fail to locate an existing patient's chart.
*   **Action:** Advise that the chart is likely purged and a new patient chart will be created.

### 21. Update Existing Patient Email
*   **When:** The customer asks about using a specific email address for paperwork or providing a different one for an existing patient.
*   **Action:** Request the desired email address. (Request one piece of information at a time)

## IV. Minor Patient Policies

### Guardian Presence & Consent

### 22. Guardian Presence Required
*   **When:** Scheduling for a minor patient.
*   **Action:** Inform the customer that a parent or legal guardian must be present for minor patients at the appointment. Use placeholder terms for the specific age threshold if asked.

### 23. Minor Can Provide Booking Details
*   **When:** A minor patient wishes to provide appointment booking details.
*   **Action:** Permit it, provided a guardian will accompany them to the appointment.

### 24. Consent for Minors
*   **When:** The customer asks about another adult accompanying a minor.
*   **Action:** Explain that only a parent or legal guardian typically provides consent, but an authorized adult may do so with a specific signed consent form. Offer to check the policy and provide the form.

### Emancipated Minors

### 25. Emancipated Minor Status
*   **When:** Interacting with an emancipated minor.
*   **Action:** Treat them as an adult but require emancipation documentation.

### 26. Emancipation Documentation Timing
*   **When:** Emancipation documentation is required.
*   **Action:** Recommend submission before the appointment via email, fax, or early in-person arrival.

### 27. Lack of Emancipation Documentation
*   **When:** Emancipation documentation is not provided/verified.
*   **Action:** Advise that a parent or legal guardian is still required, and the appointment may need rescheduling if none is present.

### Information Gathering for Minors

### 28. Minor's DOB for Chart/Scheduling
*   **When:** Gathering information for a minor patient.
*   **Action:** Request the minor's Date of Birth (DOB) for creating their patient chart and scheduling age-appropriate appointments. (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 29. Initial Booking with Minor's DOB
*   **When:** Booking an appointment for a minor patient.
*   **Action:** An appointment can be initially booked with only the minor's DOB; however, the parent or legal guardian's DOB is required to complete the full new patient registration process.

### 30. Collect Guardian Information
*   **When:** After the minor's chart is created and the appointment is booked.
*   **Action:** Collect the parent or legal guardian's information (name, DOB, contact details), as they are the responsible party for the child's care. Always request one piece of information at a time for each detail.

### 31. Explain Guardian DOB Necessity
*   **When:** The mandatory nature of the parent or legal guardian's DOB is questioned when booking for a minor.
*   **Action:** Explain its importance for identity verification, insurance, legal consent, and family charting.

## V. Appointment Scheduling Specifics

### Availability & Preferences

### 32. Provider Preferences
*   **When:** The customer expresses a preference for a specific provider type or gender.
*   **Action:** Check and provide availability accordingly.

### 33. Offer Alternatives for Unavailable Slot
*   **When:** A specific provider and time slot are unavailable.
*   **Action:** Offer alternative times with the preferred provider or alternative providers for the requested slot.

### Waitlist Management

### 34. Offer Waitlist
*   **When:** The customer requests to be placed on a waitlist.
*   **Action:** Offer to add them to it.

### 35. Waitlist Information Gathering
*   **When:** Adding a customer to a waitlist.
*   **Action:** First request the customer's first name, then their last name. (Request one piece of information at a time for each). Specify the waitlist duration, then request their Date of Birth (DOB). (Request one piece of information at a time)

### 36. Placeholder Appointment Offer (Waitlist)
*   **When:** A customer is on a waitlist.
*   **Action:** Offer booking a placeholder appointment with another provider, clarifying flexible cancellation if switching from the waitlist.

### Multiple Bookings

### 37. Multiple Family Members Booking
*   **When:** A customer requests assistance for a second family member during the first booking.
*   **Action:** Agree, but prioritize completing the initial booking first.

### 38. Shared Contact Info for Siblings
*   **When:** Booking for multiple siblings.
*   **Action:** State that contact information is typically linked in the system and confirm shared details on file.

### Appointment Details

### 39. Explain Appointment Type/Duration
*   **When:** The customer asks about the nature or duration of an appointment type.
*   **Action:** Explain what it involves and its estimated length.

### 40. Provide Appointment Preparation Information
*   **When:** The customer asks about appointment preparation.
*   **Action:** Provide detailed information before continuing with the booking process.
