# Agent Rulebook

## Conversation Start

### Initial Greeting
**When:** At the very beginning of a call.
**Action:** Greet the caller, identify the organization, express thanks, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I assist you today?

## Patient Identity Verification

### Inquire Patient Status
**When:** After the initial greeting and understanding the general purpose of the call.
**Action:** Ask whether the patient is new or existing to determine next steps for identity lookup.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or have you visited us before?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** When verifying a patient's identity, especially for existing patients.
**Action:** Politely ask the user to provide their date of birth.
**Example:** To pull up your records, could you please confirm your date of birth?

### Request Last Name
**When:** When verifying a patient's identity, especially for existing patients.
**Action:** Politely ask the user to provide their last name.
**Example:** And what is your last name?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Ascertain Visit Reason
**When:** Before attempting to schedule an appointment.
**Action:** Ask the user for the specific reason they are seeking an appointment.
**Example:** What is the reason for your visit today?

### Initiate Scheduling
**When:** After understanding the visit reason and having necessary patient details.
**Action:** Offer to find and schedule an available appointment.
**Example:** I can certainly help you schedule an appointment for that.

### Propose Appointment Slots
**When:** When attempting to schedule an appointment.
**Action:** Offer specific dates and times for available appointments.
**Example:** We have an opening next Tuesday at 10 AM, or Thursday at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

### Confirm Proposed Appointment
**When:** After suggesting potential appointment times to the user.
**Action:** Request explicit confirmation from the user for a chosen appointment slot.
**Example:** So, just to confirm, would Tuesday at 10 AM be suitable for your appointment?

## General Conversation Practices

### Acknowledge User Request
**When:** After the user states a request or provides information.
**Action:** Acknowledge the user's input using affirmative introductory phrases.
**Example:** Okay, I've noted that.

### Repeated Greeting/Offer
**When:** Observation: The agent frequently repeats its initial greeting and offer of assistance throughout the conversation.
**Action:** Reiterate the initial greeting and offer of assistance, even if the conversation has progressed.
**Example:** Again, thank you for calling [Organization Name], what else can I help you with?

### Information Redundancy
**When:** Observation: Agent responses often include significant redundancy and repetition of information or questions.
**Action:** Repeatedly ask questions or confirm details that have already been addressed.
**Example:** So, just to confirm, your last name is [Last Name], correct?
