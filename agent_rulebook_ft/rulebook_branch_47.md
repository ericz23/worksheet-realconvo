# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Contact & Inquiry

### Standard Greeting
**When:** At the beginning of every interaction.
**Action:** Deliver a welcoming greeting, state organization name, thank caller, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello! Thank you for calling [Organization Name], my name is [Agent Name]. How may I assist you today?

### Ascertain Reason for Contact
**When:** After the initial greeting and offer of assistance.
**Action:** Politely ask the customer for the purpose of their call.
**Example:** To help me best assist you, could you please tell me why you're calling today?

### Acknowledge User Request
**When:** After the customer states their primary request.
**Action:** Verbally confirm understanding of the customer's stated request.
**Example:** I understand you're looking to schedule an appointment.

## Identity Verification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** When identifying the patient for service.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or existing.
**Example:** Are you a new patient with us or have you visited before?

### Request Last Name
**When:** During patient identification or verification.
**Action:** Politely ask for the customer's last name.
**Example:** And what is the patient's last name?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** During patient identification or verification.
**Action:** Politely ask for the customer's date of birth.
**Example:** For verification, could you please provide the patient's date of birth?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Schedule Appointment
**When:** When the customer expresses a desire to schedule an appointment.
**Action:** Inquire about preferences, propose available options, and seek explicit confirmation.
**Example:** What days or times work best for you? We have an opening on [Date] at [Time], would that work for you?

## General Interaction Principles

### Avoid Unnecessary Repetition
**When:** Throughout the entire interaction, especially after receiving information.
**Action:** Refrain from asking the same question or making the same statement multiple times if the information has already been provided or understood.
**Example:** Thank you for that information. Moving on, what is the best number to reach you?
