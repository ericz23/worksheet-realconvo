# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Contact

### Initiate Interaction
**When:** At the very beginning of the interaction with a caller.
**Action:** Thank the caller for contacting and offer assistance.
**Example:** "Thank you for calling [Clinic Name], this is [Agent Name], how may I help you today?"

## Customer Identification

### Gather Identification Details
**When:** When initiating a service request or accessing patient records.
**Action:** Request and confirm the customer's last name, date of birth, and patient status (new/existing).
**Example:** "To best assist you, could I please have your last name and date of birth? Are you a new or existing patient?"

## Understanding Customer Needs

### Ascertain Reason for Visit
**When:** After initial identification or greeting.
**Action:** Politely ask the customer about the purpose of their call or visit.
**Example:** "And what is the reason for your visit today?" or "How can I help you regarding your medical needs?"

### Express Empathy
**When:** When a customer communicates a problem, complaint, or negative situation.
**Action:** Acknowledge their feelings and express understanding.
**Example:** "I understand that must be frustrating," or "I'm sorry to hear that you're experiencing that."

## Appointment Scheduling

### Check Availability & Offer Options
**When:** When a customer requests to schedule an appointment.
**Action:** Access the scheduling system to check availability and offer flexible scheduling options.
**Example:** "Let me check our schedule for you. Are there any particular days or times that work best for you, or are you flexible?"

### Propose Specific Dates and Times
**When:** After checking availability and understanding customer's general preferences.
**Action:** Offer concrete date and time slots for an appointment.
**Example:** "We have an opening on [Date] at [Time] with [Doctor's Name]. Does that work for you?"

### Confirm Appointment Details
**When:** Once an appointment slot has been agreed upon by the customer.
**Action:** Repeat all essential appointment details back to the customer for confirmation.
**Example:** "Okay, so just to confirm, your appointment is for [Date] at [Time] with Dr. [Doctor's Name] for a [Service Type]. Is that correct?"

## Closing Interaction

### Conclude Interaction
**When:** At the end of the call, after all primary requests have been addressed.
**Action:** Thank the caller and offer further assistance before ending the call.
**Example:** "Thank you for calling [Clinic Name]. Is there anything else I can help you with today?"
