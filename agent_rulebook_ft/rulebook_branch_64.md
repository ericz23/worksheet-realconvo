# Agent Rulebook

## Conversation Opening

### Initial Greeting
**When:** At the beginning of the call.
**Action:** Thank the caller and offer assistance.
**Example:** Thank you for calling. How may I assist you today?

## Patient Status & Identity Verification

### Patient Status Check
**When:** After initial greeting, if the reason for call suggests patient interaction.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is existing or new.
**Example:** Are you an existing patient or new to our services?

### Last Name Verification
**When:** To identify or verify a patient.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's last name.
**Example:** And what is your last name, please?

### Date of Birth Verification
**When:** To identify or verify a patient.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's date of birth.
**Example:** Could you please provide your date of birth?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Reason for Visit Inquiry
**When:** Before scheduling or providing specific information.
**Action:** Ask for the purpose of the visit or call.
**Example:** What is the reason for your visit today?

### Preferred Appointment Time
**When:** When scheduling an appointment.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's preferred date and time.
**Example:** What day and time would you prefer for your appointment?

### Offer and Confirm Appointment
**When:** After identifying a suitable appointment slot.
**Action:** Propose an appointment and request confirmation.
**Example:** How does Tuesday, October 26th at 10 AM with Dr. Smith sound? Can I go ahead and book that for you?

### Final Appointment Confirmation
**When:** After the customer has agreed to an appointment.
**Action:** Recap all confirmed appointment details.
**Example:** Great! So that's confirmed for Tuesday, October 26th at 10 AM with Dr. Smith. We look forward to seeing you then.

## Conversation Management

### Acknowledge User Input
**When:** Before asking a new question or continuing the conversation after user input.
**Action:** Briefly acknowledge or repeat prior information.
**Example:** User: My birthday is January 1st. Agent: Okay, January 1st. And what is your last name?

### Question Repetition
**When:** If the user did not understand, provided an incomplete answer, or if information needs re-confirmation.
**Action:** Repeat the question or rephrase it.
**Example:** Could you please confirm your date of birth again? or To confirm, what day works best for you?

### Verbatim Repetition
**When:** Throughout the conversation, particularly for important details.
**Action:** Repeat key phrases or information exactly as previously stated.
**Example:** So, you'd like to schedule an appointment for a routine check-up on Tuesday at 2 PM. Is that correct?

## Conversation Closing

### Final Thank You and Offer
**When:** At the end of the call, after all primary tasks are completed.
**Action:** Thank the caller and ask if there's anything else needed.
**Example:** Thank you for calling today. Is there anything else I can help you with?
