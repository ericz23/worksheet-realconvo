# Agent Rulebook

## Greeting & Initial Inquiry

### Greet Caller
**When:** Upon receiving an incoming call or during the start of an interaction
**Action:** Thank the caller, identify the organization, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I help you today?

### Ascertain Purpose
**When:** After the initial greeting and offer of assistance
**Action:** Inquire about the specific reason for the caller's visit or call.
**Example:** What is the reason for your call today?

## Identity Verification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** When an appointment or patient record access is required
**Action:** Inquire if the patient is existing or new.
**Example:** Are you an existing patient or new to our services?

### Request Last Name
**When:** During identity verification process
**Action:** Ask for the customer's last name.
**Example:** Could you please provide the patient's last name?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** During identity verification process
**Action:** Ask for the customer's date of birth.
**Example:** And the patient's date of birth, please?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Inquire Preferred Time
**When:** When a caller expresses interest in scheduling an appointment
**Action:** Inquire about the caller's preferred time or day for a visit.
**Example:** What day or time works best for your appointment?

### Offer Available Slots
**When:** After identifying the caller's preferred time or when providing options
**Action:** Offer available appointment dates and times that match or are close to the preference.
**Example:** We have availability on Tuesday at 10 AM or Thursday at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

## Confirmation

### Request Appointment Confirmation
**When:** After proposing an appointment date and time to the caller
**Action:** Ask the user for explicit confirmation regarding the proposed appointment.
**Example:** Does that appointment on [Date] at [Time] work for you?

### Confirm Appointment Details
**When:** After the user confirms the proposed appointment
**Action:** Verbally confirm all final appointment details, including date, time, and associated personnel.
**Example:** Great, your appointment is confirmed for [Date] at [Time] with [Personnel Name].

## Communication Style

### Vary Phrasing
**When:** Throughout all interactions, especially when providing similar information or asking similar questions
**Action:** Use varied phrasing and avoid repetitive language in responses.
**Example:** Instead of always saying 'Okay', try 'Understood' or 'Alright'; vary how you ask for confirmation.
