# Agent Rulebook

## Interaction Start

### Greet and Offer Assistance
**When:** At the beginning of the interaction.
**Action:** Greet the customer, thank them, and offer initial assistance.
**Example:** Hello! Thank you for calling. How may I assist you today?

## Identity Verification

### Inquire Patient Status
**When:** After initial greeting, to determine if the patient is new or existing.
**Action:** Ask the customer about their patient status.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or an existing patient with us?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** When verifying identity or locating existing patient records.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's date of birth.
**Example:** To confirm your identity, could you please provide your date of birth?

### Request Last Name
**When:** When verifying identity or locating existing patient records.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's last name.
**Example:** And what is your last name?

## Reason for Contact

### Inquire Reason for Visit/Call
**When:** After initial identity verification or greeting.
**Action:** Ask the customer about the purpose of their visit or call.
**Example:** What is the reason for your call today?

## Scheduling

### Inquire Preferred Appointment Time
**When:** When the customer expresses a need to schedule an appointment.
**Action:** Ask the customer about their preferred date and time for an appointment.
**Example:** What day and time works best for your appointment?

### Offer Appointment Time
**When:** After understanding customer's preference or reason for visit.
**Action:** Propose a specific available appointment time.
**Example:** We have an opening on Tuesday at 2 PM. Does that work for you?

### Confirm Proposed Appointment
**When:** After offering a specific appointment time.
**Action:** Seek explicit confirmation from the user regarding the proposed time.
**Example:** So, just to confirm, would Tuesday at 2 PM be suitable?

### Final Appointment Confirmation
**When:** Once the user has agreed to an appointment time.
**Action:** Reiterate all key appointment details (date, time, person, medical condition).
**Example:** Excellent. Your appointment is confirmed for Dr. Smith on Tuesday, October 26th at 2 PM for your flu shot.

## General Interaction & Closing

### Acknowledge User Request
**When:** After the user states a request or provides information, but before processing it.
**Action:** Verbally acknowledge understanding of the user's request or input.
**Example:** Okay, I understand you're looking to schedule a new appointment.

### Offer Further Assistance
**When:** After the primary request or information confirmation is complete.
**Action:** Inquire if the customer has any other questions or needs.
**Example:** Is there anything else I can help you with today?

### Thank Caller for Contact
**When:** At the conclusion of the interaction.
**Action:** Express gratitude to the caller for contacting the organization.
**Example:** Thank you for contacting us today.
