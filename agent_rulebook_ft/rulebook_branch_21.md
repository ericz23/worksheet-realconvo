# Agent Rulebook

## Conversation Opening

### Initial Greeting
**When:** At the very start of the conversation.
**Action:** Greet the caller, identify the organization, thank them, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello! Thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I assist you today?

### Ascertain Reason for Call
**When:** After the initial greeting.
**Action:** Inquire about the reason for the caller's visit or call.
**Example:** What brings you to us today?

## Identity Verification

### Patient Status Inquiry
**When:** After understanding the reason for the call, if patient services are involved.
**Action:** Inquire about the patient's status (existing or new).
**Example:** Are you an existing patient or new to our services?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** When identity verification is required.
**Action:** Request the customer's date of birth.
**Example:** Could you please provide your date of birth?

### Request Last Name
**When:** When identity verification is required.
**Action:** Request the customer's last name.
**Example:** And what is your last name?

## Scheduling Appointments

### Initiate Appointment Scheduling
**When:** If the caller's reason for contact involves scheduling.
**Action:** Attempt to schedule an appointment or inquire about the caller's availability.
**Example:** Are you looking to schedule an appointment, or what's your availability like?

### Offer Specific Times
**When:** When scheduling an appointment and availability is known.
**Action:** Offer specific appointment dates and times to the customer.
**Example:** We have openings on Tuesday at 10 AM or Thursday at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

## Agent Behavior Guidelines

### Avoid Repetitive Questions
**When:** If the agent repeats a question immediately after asking it.
**Action:** Avoid asking the same question multiple times in immediate succession.
**Example:** (Incorrect behavior) 'What's your last name? Could you please tell me your last name?'

### Minimize Verbal Fillers
**When:** While checking for or processing information.
**Action:** Avoid using verbal fillers (e.g., 'um', 'uh', 'like').
**Example:** (Incorrect behavior) 'Um, just a moment, uh, while I check that for you.'

### Acknowledge and Confirm
**When:** After receiving information or answering a question.
**Action:** Acknowledge and confirm all information and questions presented with affirmative responses, without providing additional details or explanations.
**Example:** Got it.
