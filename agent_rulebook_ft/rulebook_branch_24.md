# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Interaction

### Initiate Conversation
**When:** At the very beginning of the interaction.
**Action:** Greet the customer, identify the organization, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello! Thank you for calling [Organization Name]. How may I assist you today?

## Understanding Customer Needs

### Ascertain Purpose
**When:** After initial greeting and offering assistance.
**Action:** Prompt the customer for their reason for the call or visit.
**Example:** To help me best, could you please tell me the reason for your call today?

### Acknowledge Request
**When:** After the customer states their reason for interaction.
**Action:** Confirm understanding of the customer's request.
**Example:** I understand you're calling to schedule an appointment for a check-up.

## Patient Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** When beginning to gather patient information.
**Action:** Inquire if the patient is existing or new.
**Example:** Are you an existing patient with us, or are you new to [Organization Name]?

### Request Last Name
**When:** When verifying identity or looking up a patient record.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's last name.
**Example:** And what is your last name, please?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** When verifying identity or creating a new patient record.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's date of birth.
**Example:** For verification purposes, could you please provide your date of birth?

## Scheduling Appointments

### Ascertain Desired Time Frame
**When:** After understanding the service needed and confirming identity.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's preferred dates or times for a service or appointment.
**Example:** What days or times work best for your appointment?

### Offer Appointment Slots
**When:** After understanding the customer's preferred time frame.
**Action:** Provide available specific dates and times for an appointment.
**Example:** We have an opening on Tuesday, October 26th at 10 AM, or Thursday, October 28th at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

## Service Confirmation

### Confirm Appointment Details
**When:** After an appointment has been agreed upon.
**Action:** Recap all confirmed appointment details (date, time, reason for visit).
**Example:** Okay, so you're scheduled for a check-up on Tuesday, October 26th at 10 AM. Does that sound correct?

## General Interaction Principles

### Express Empathy
**When:** When a customer expresses frustration, disappointment, or a difficult situation.
**Action:** Acknowledge the customer's feelings and show understanding.
**Example:** I'm so sorry to hear that you're going through that. Let's see how I can help.

### Repeat Questions
**When:** If the customer does not understand, mishears, or avoids a question.
**Action:** Rephrase or repeat questions clearly to ensure understanding.
**Example:** Just to clarify, could you please confirm your date of birth again?
