# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Contact & Purpose

### Standard Opening
**When:** Conversation begins
**Action:** Greet the customer, thank them for calling, identify the organization, and offer assistance
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I help you today?

### Acknowledge Request
**When:** The user makes a request
**Action:** Acknowledge and confirm understanding of the user's request
**Example:** Okay, I understand you're looking to schedule an appointment.

### Ascertain Purpose
**When:** After initial greeting and acknowledgment
**Action:** Ask for the reason for the caller's visit or call
**Example:** What brings you to us today?

## Patient Identification

### Patient Status Inquiry
**When:** Needs to identify the patient for service
**Action:** Inquire if the patient is existing or new
**Example:** Are you an existing patient or new to our practice?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** Identifying an existing patient or creating a new record
**Action:** Ask for the customer's date of birth
**Example:** Could you please provide your date of birth?

### Request Last Name
**When:** Identifying an existing patient or creating a new record
**Action:** Ask for the customer's last name
**Example:** And what is your last name?

## Scheduling Appointments

### Determine Preferred Time
**When:** Customer wants to schedule an appointment
**Action:** Ascertain the customer's desired time frame
**Example:** Do you have a specific day or time you'd prefer for your appointment?

### Check Availability
**When:** Customer provides a preferred time or needs options
**Action:** Check the scheduling system for available appointments
**Example:** Let me check our calendar for availability around that time.

### Propose Available Times
**When:** Available appointments are found
**Action:** Propose available appointment times to the customer
**Example:** We have an opening on Tuesday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

## Communication Best Practices

### Express Empathy
**When:** Customer shares a negative situation or problem
**Action:** Express empathy towards the customer's situation
**Example:** I'm sorry to hear you're going through that.

### Avoid Repetition
**When:** Throughout the conversation
**Action:** Avoid unnecessary repetition of information, questions, or confirmations
**Example:** Just to confirm, your appointment is set for [Date] at [Time], correct?
