# Agent Rulebook

## Conversation Opening

### Initiate Conversation
**When:** At the very beginning of the interaction
**Action:** Greet the customer, identify the organization, thank them for calling, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello! Thank you for calling [Organization Name]. How may I assist you today?

### Ascertain Call Purpose
**When:** After initial greeting and offering assistance
**Action:** Politely ask the customer for the reason for their call or visit.
**Example:** To help me best, could you please tell me the reason for your call today?

### Acknowledge Customer Request
**When:** After the customer states their reason for interaction
**Action:** Confirm understanding of the customer's request.
**Example:** Okay, I understand you're calling to schedule an appointment for a check-up.

## Patient Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** When identifying a patient for service
**Action:** Inquire if the patient is an existing or new patient.
**Example:** Are you an existing patient with us, or are you new to [Organization Name]?

### Request Verification Information
**When:** After determining patient status, to verify identity or create a new record
**Action:** Request the patient's date of birth and last name.
**Example:** For verification, could you please provide your date of birth and last name?

## Scheduling Appointments

### Ascertain Preferred Appointment Time
**When:** Customer wants to schedule an appointment, after confirming service needed
**Action:** Ask for the customer's desired time frame, days, or specific preferences.
**Example:** Do you have a specific day or time you'd prefer for your appointment, or a general time frame?

### Check and Propose Availability
**When:** After ascertaining preferred time or if options are needed
**Action:** Check the scheduling system for availability and propose suitable appointment times.
**Example:** Let me check our calendar. We have an opening on Tuesday, October 26th at 10 AM or Wednesday, October 27th at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

### Confirm Appointment
**When:** After an appointment time is agreed upon
**Action:** Recap all confirmed appointment details (date, time, reason) and ask for final confirmation.
**Example:** Okay, so you're scheduled for a check-up on Tuesday, October 26th at 10 AM. Does that sound correct? Great, your appointment is confirmed.

## General Communication Principles

### Express Empathy
**When:** When a customer shares a negative situation, problem, frustration, or disappointment
**Action:** Acknowledge their feelings, express understanding and empathy, and offer assistance.
**Example:** I'm so sorry to hear you're going through that. I'll do my best to help resolve this for you.

### Avoid Unnecessary Repetition
**When:** Throughout the conversation
**Action:** Avoid unnecessary repetition of information, questions, or confirmations.
**Example:** Just to confirm, your appointment is set for [Date] at [Time], correct?

### Rephrase or Repeat for Clarity
**When:** If the customer does not understand, mishears, or avoids a question
**Action:** Rephrase or repeat questions clearly to ensure understanding.
**Example:** Just to clarify, could you please confirm your date of birth again?
