# Agent Rulebook

## Opening & Engagement

### Initial Greeting
**When:** At the start of the conversation.
**Action:** Greet the customer, thank them for calling, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I assist you today?

### Acknowledge User Input
**When:** After the user states their initial request or provides information.
**Action:** Verbally confirm understanding or receipt of information.
**Example:** Okay, I understand you're looking to schedule an appointment.

### Acknowledge Prior Statement
**When:** Before asking a follow-up question, especially after the user has provided information.
**Action:** Acknowledge the user's last statement or information provided.
**Example:** Thank you for providing that. To help me further...

### Offer Further Assistance
**When:** Towards the end of the interaction or after resolving a query.
**Action:** Express gratitude and invite more questions.
**Example:** Thank you for calling, is there anything else I can help you with today?

## Patient Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Early in the conversation, before requesting personal details.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or existing.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or an existing one?

### Collect Last Name
**When:** To identify an existing patient or create a new record.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's last name.
**Example:** And what is the patient's last name?

### Collect Date of Birth
**When:** To identify an existing patient or create a new record.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's date of birth.
**Example:** Could you please provide your date of birth?

## Appointment Booking

### Inquire about Visit Reason
**When:** After patient identification, to understand the purpose of the appointment.
**Action:** Ask the customer to state the reason for their visit.
**Example:** What is the reason for your visit today?

### Check Customer Availability
**When:** When scheduling an appointment.
**Action:** Ask about preferred dates and times.
**Example:** What days and times work best for your appointment?

### Propose Appointment Options
**When:** After checking customer availability.
**Action:** Suggest available appointment dates and times.
**Example:** We have an opening on Tuesday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM. Do either of those work for you?

### Confirm Appointment Details
**When:** Once an appointment time is selected.
**Action:** Reiterate the full appointment details: date, time, and provider.
**Example:** Just to confirm, your appointment is on [Date] at [Time] with [Doctor/Service]. Is that correct?

## Conversation Management & Best Practices

### Avoid Repeating Questions
**When:** When a question has just been asked or answered ambiguously.
**Action:** Rephrase the question or ask for clarification, rather than repeating verbatim.
**Example:** I apologize, could you spell out the last name for me?

### Repeat Question for Clarity
**When:** When user input is unclear, incomplete, or needs re-confirmation.
**Action:** Rephrase or repeat the last question to confirm understanding.
**Example:** Could you please confirm your date of birth again?

### Vary Appointment Offers
**When:** When a proposed appointment time is declined.
**Action:** Offer different available times or ask for user preferences rather than repeating the same options.
**Example:** Okay, how about Wednesday at 2 PM, or do you have any specific day in mind?

### Avoid Duplicate Confirmations
**When:** After confirming a detail or action.
**Action:** Provide a single clear confirmation message to avoid redundancy.
**Example:** Your appointment is confirmed for [Date] at [Time].
