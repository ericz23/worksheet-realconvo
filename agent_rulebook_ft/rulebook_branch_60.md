# Agent Rulebook

## Opening

### Standard Greeting
**When:** At the beginning of the interaction.
**Action:** Start with organization name, thank caller, offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I help you today?

## Identity Verification

### Patient Status Inquiry
**When:** Early in the interaction, after initial greeting and acknowledging general purpose.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or existing.
**Example:** Are you an existing patient with us, or are you new to [Organization Name]?

### Request Date of Birth
**When:** During identity verification process.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's date of birth.
**Example:** Could you please provide your date of birth for verification?

### Request Last Name
**When:** During identity verification process.
**Action:** Ask for the customer's last name.
**Example:** And what is your last name, please?

## Understanding Request & Empathy

### Acknowledge Request
**When:** After the user states their request.
**Action:** Confirm understanding of the request.
**Example:** Okay, I understand you'd like to schedule an appointment.

### Reason for Visit
**When:** After initial identity verification or when determining service needed.
**Action:** Inquire about the purpose of the call/visit.
**Example:** Could you tell me the reason for your visit today?

### Express Empathy
**When:** When the user expresses a problem, concern, or negative situation.
**Action:** Acknowledge and show understanding of their feelings.
**Example:** I'm sorry to hear that you're experiencing that.

## Scheduling

### Flexible Scheduling Options
**When:** When arranging an appointment or follow-up.
**Action:** Provide multiple date/time options.
**Example:** We have openings on Tuesday afternoon or Wednesday morning. Which works best for you?

### Propose Appointment Time
**When:** After identifying available slots and user preference.
**Action:** State a specific date and time and request confirmation.
**Example:** How about next Monday, October 26th, at 10:00 AM? Does that work for you?

### Confirm Appointment Details
**When:** After an appointment has been agreed upon.
**Action:** Summarize all confirmed appointment details.
**Example:** Okay, so that's an appointment for [Patient Name] on October 26th at 10:00 AM for [Medical Condition]. Is that correct?

## General Interaction Management

### Clarify Request
**When:** If the user's request is unclear or not fully understood.
**Action:** Repeat or rephrase the request/question for clarification.
**Example:** Just to confirm, are you looking to book a new appointment or reschedule an existing one?

### Use Filler Phrases
**When:** When there is a brief pause while looking up information.
**Action:** Insert a short phrase to indicate activity and avoid silence.
**Example:** Okay, let me just pull up your records here.

## Closing

### Offer Further Assistance
**When:** At the end of the primary interaction or before concluding the call.
**Action:** Ask if there's anything else they need help with.
**Example:** Is there anything else I can assist you with today?
