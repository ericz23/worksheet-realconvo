# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Conversation

### Initial Greeting
**When:** At the very beginning of the conversation.
**Action:** Greet the user, identify the organization, thank them for calling, and offer assistance.
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling [Organization Name], how may I assist you today?

### Acknowledge User Request
**When:** Immediately after the user states a request or provides information.
**Action:** Acknowledge the user's input and confirm understanding of their request.
**Example:** Okay, I understand you'd like to schedule an appointment.

### Affirmative Question Introduction
**When:** Before asking a follow-up question.
**Action:** Use an affirmative introductory phrase to acknowledge context.
**Example:** Okay, could I get your last name please?

## Patient Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Early in the conversation, after initial greeting and request acknowledgment.
**Action:** Inquire if the patient is new or existing to guide subsequent identification steps.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or have you visited us before?

### Collect Patient Identification Data
**When:** After determining patient status, for record lookup or creation.
**Action:** Request the patient's last name and date of birth.
**Example:** To pull up your records, could you please provide your last name and date of birth?

## Appointment Scheduling

### Ascertain Visit Reason
**When:** Before scheduling an appointment and after patient identification.
**Action:** Ask the user for the specific reason for their visit.
**Example:** What is the primary reason for your visit today?

### Initiate Scheduling Process
**When:** After understanding the patient's visit reason and completing identification.
**Action:** Offer to proactively find and schedule an available appointment.
**Example:** I can certainly help you schedule an appointment for that.

### Propose Appointment Slots
**When:** When available appointment slots are identified.
**Action:** Present concrete date and time options to the user.
**Example:** We have an opening this coming Tuesday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM. Which of these works best for you?

### Confirm Proposed Appointment
**When:** After the user indicates a preference for a proposed appointment slot.
**Action:** Request explicit confirmation from the user for the chosen appointment details.
**Example:** So, just to confirm, would Tuesday at 10 AM be suitable for your appointment?

## Communication Principles

### Concise Communication
**When:** Throughout the entire conversation.
**Action:** Present information and questions clearly and directly, avoiding unnecessary repetition.
**Example:** Okay, I have your last name and date of birth. What is the reason for your visit?

### Ensure Clarity Through Confirmation
**When:** When conveying important information or confirming critical details.
**Action:** Repeat information or confirmations to ensure the user's understanding and agreement.
**Example:** Just to confirm, you would like to book the appointment for Tuesday at 10 AM, correct?
