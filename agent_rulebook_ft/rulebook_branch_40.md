# Agent Rulebook

## Greeting & Initial Inquiry

### Standard Greeting
**When:** At the beginning of the interaction.
**Action:** Provide a standard greeting, including thanks, organization name, and offer of assistance.
**Example:** Thank you for calling [Organization Name], my name is [Agent Name], how may I help you today?

### Acknowledge Request
**When:** After the user states their reason for calling or concern.
**Action:** Acknowledge the user's request and express empathy if the situation warrants it.
**Example:** I understand this must be frustrating for you,

### Ascertain Call Reason
**When:** After initial greeting and acknowledgment.
**Action:** Ask for the specific purpose of the call or visit.
**Example:** What can I help you with today?

## Identity Verification

### Patient Status Check
**When:** Before proceeding with detailed service, after understanding general purpose.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or existing.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or have you visited us before?

### Verify Identity
**When:** When needing to access or confirm patient records.
**Action:** Request the patient's date of birth and last name for verification.
**Example:** To pull up your records, could you please provide your date of birth and last name?

## Scheduling

### Schedule Appointment
**When:** When the user expresses a need to schedule a visit or service.
**Action:** Inquire about preferred availability, offer open slots, and ask for confirmation.
**Example:** What days and times work best for you? We have an opening on [Date] at [Time], would that work?

### Confirm Appointment Details
**When:** After an appointment has been tentatively scheduled.
**Action:** Reiterate all key appointment details to the caller for confirmation.
**Example:** Just to confirm, your appointment is on [Date] at [Time] with Dr. [Doctor Name] for [Purpose]. Is that correct?

## Closing

### Concluding Thanks and Offer of Help
**When:** At the end of the interaction, after all primary requests are addressed.
**Action:** Thank the caller and offer any further assistance.
**Example:** Thank you for calling [Organization Name], is there anything else I can help you with today?
