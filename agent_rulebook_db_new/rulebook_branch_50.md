# Agent Rulebook

## Appointment Initiation & Patient Status

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Patient initiates an appointment booking.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited before?

### Acknowledge Patient Status
**When:** Patient confirms their new or established status.
**Action:** Acknowledge the patient's confirmed status.
**Example:** Okay, thank you for confirming you're an established patient.

## Identity Verification & Information Retrieval

### Request Identifying Information (Established)
**When:** Patient is identified as established.
**Action:** Request specific identifying information (e.g., phone number, DOB) to locate their chart.
**Example:** To find your chart, could I please get your phone number or date of birth?

### Acknowledge Information Receipt
**When:** Patient provides identifying information.
**Action:** Verbally acknowledge receipt of the information.
**Example:** Thank you, I have that.

### Inform of Chart Lookup
**When:** Agent is about to access patient information.
**Action:** Inform the patient about the information lookup and a potential brief wait.
**Example:** Please hold for a moment while I pull up your records.

## Appointment Purpose & Details

### Confirm Purpose & Reason for Visit
**When:** Patient information has been successfully retrieved.
**Action:** Confirm the call's purpose and inquire about the specific type or reason for the appointment.
**Example:** Now that I have your chart, you're looking to book an appointment, correct? What's the reason for your visit today?

## Scheduling

### Offer Available Appointment Times
**When:** The reason for the appointment is clear.
**Action:** Present the patient with available appointment slots.
**Example:** For that, we have an opening next Monday at 9 AM or Wednesday at 1 PM. Do either of those work?

### Suggest Alternative Times
**When:** Initial appointment offer is not suitable for the patient.
**Action:** Propose alternative appointment times.
**Example:** No problem at all. How about later next week, perhaps Thursday at 10 AM, or Friday in the afternoon?
