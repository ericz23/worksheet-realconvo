# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Type Identification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Initial contact with patient or when identifying who the appointment is for.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Are you a new patient with us, or have you visited before?

## New Patient Information Gathering

### Gather New Patient Demographics & Needs
**When:** Patient identifies as new.
**Action:** Request their full name, desired service or department, last medical checkup details, and if any family members have previously scheduled.
**Example:** Okay, as a new patient, could you please tell me your full name, what service or department you're looking for, when your last medical checkup was, and if any family members have previously scheduled with us?

## New Patient Insurance Information

### Inquire About Dental Insurance
**When:** Patient identifies as new and after initial demographic gathering.
**Action:** Ask if the patient has dental insurance.
**Example:** Do you happen to have dental insurance?

### Request Insurance Provider Name
**When:** Patient indicates they have dental insurance.
**Action:** Ask for the name of their insurance provider.
**Example:** Great, could you please tell me the name of your dental insurance provider?

### Clarify Insurance Plan Type
**When:** Patient provides insurance provider name.
**Action:** Ask if the plan is a PPO or HMO.
**Example:** And is that a PPO or an HMO plan?

### Acknowledge No Dental Insurance
**When:** Patient states they do not have dental insurance.
**Action:** Acknowledge the information and proceed with scheduling or other relevant steps.
**Example:** Okay, thank you for letting me know. We can still proceed with scheduling your appointment.

## Appointment Scope

### Determine Appointment Participants
**When:** After identifying patient type and gathering initial details for scheduling.
**Action:** Ask if the appointment is for the caller only or includes family members.
**Example:** Is this appointment just for you, or will it be for other family members as well?

## General Communication & Clarification

### Seek Clarification
**When:** Information provided by the patient is unclear, incomplete, or misunderstood by the agent.
**Action:** Politely ask for clarification, repeat details to confirm understanding, or ask the patient to rephrase.
**Example:** Just to clarify, did you say your last name was 'Smith' or 'Smyth'? Could you spell that for me?

### Use Acknowledgments
**When:** Patient provides information, answers a question, or confirms understanding.
**Action:** Use short verbal acknowledgments (e.g., 'Okay', 'Got it', 'Alright') to confirm receipt of information and keep the conversation flowing.
**Example:** Okay, I have that down. Thank you.
