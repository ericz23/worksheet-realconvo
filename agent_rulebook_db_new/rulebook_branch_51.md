# Agent Rulebook

## Initial Contact and Identity Verification

### Confirm Patient Status and Identity
**When:** Initial contact with patient.
**Action:** Inquire about new/existing status and ask for basic identifying information (e.g., name, date of birth).
**Example:** Hello, thank you for calling. Are you a new or existing patient with us today? Could I get your full name and date of birth?

### Request Info for Established Patient Chart
**When:** Patient identifies as established.
**Action:** Ask for a specific identifier to pull up their chart.
**Example:** Since you're an existing patient, could I please have your phone number or date of birth to pull up your chart?

### Acknowledge Patient Status and Info Receipt
**When:** Patient has provided status and identifying information.
**Action:** Verbally confirm understanding of their status and receipt of data.
**Example:** Okay, thank you. You're an existing patient and I have your phone number.

### Inform and Acknowledge Chart Access
**When:** Before accessing patient's chart.
**Action:** Tell the patient you're accessing their information and manage their wait.
**Example:** Please bear with me for a moment while I access your chart.

### Verify Identity Against Chart Data
**When:** After patient chart is open.
**Action:** Cross-reference provided information with chart data to confirm identity.
**Example:** Just to confirm, your address on file is [address], is that correct?

## Understanding Patient Request

### Confirm Call Purpose and Details
**When:** After identity verification.
**Action:** Elicit the reason for the call and gather necessary context.
**Example:** Now that I have your chart open, how can I help you today? What is the reason for your call?

### State Immediate Next Action
**When:** After understanding the patient's request.
**Action:** Clearly communicate the next step in resolving their issue.
**Example:** Okay, I understand. I will now look for available appointments for you.

### Ask Clarifying Questions
**When:** Ambiguity in patient information or request.
**Action:** Pose specific questions to resolve confusion.
**Example:** Just to be clear, are you looking for an appointment for yourself or for a family member?

## Appointment Management

### Offer and Confirm Appointment Slots
**When:** During appointment scheduling.
**Action:** Provide concrete appointment options and wait for patient's selection.
**Example:** We have an opening on Tuesday at 10 AM or Wednesday at 2 PM. Which works best for you?

### Offer Alternative Appointment Options
**When:** Patient declines initial appointment options.
**Action:** Find and propose different available times.
**Example:** No problem at all. How about later in the week, say Thursday afternoon, or next Monday morning?
