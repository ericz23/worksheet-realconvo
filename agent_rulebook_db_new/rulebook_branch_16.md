# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Identification

### Identify Patient Status
**When:** Initial patient interaction or context requires patient status clarification.
**Action:** Ask the patient if they are new or established.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or have you visited us before?

## Insurance Verification (New Patients)

### Inquire about Dental Insurance
**When:** Patient identifies as new.
**Action:** Ask if the patient has dental insurance.
**Example:** Okay, as a new patient, do you have dental insurance?

### Determine Insurance Plan Type
**When:** Patient confirms having dental insurance.
**Action:** Ask for the insurance plan type (e.g., PPO/HMO).
**Example:** Great, is your plan a PPO or an HMO?

## Handling Insurance Uncertainty

### Offer Insurance Information Assistance
**When:** Patient expresses uncertainty about insurance details (plan type or office assignment).
**Action:** Offer to help identify the necessary information.
**Example:** No worries at all, I can help you figure out those details.

### Suggest Insurance Information Sources
**When:** Patient expresses uncertainty about insurance details (plan type or office assignment).
**Action:** Suggest checking the insurance card or the provider's website for plan details or office assignment.
**Example:** You can often find that information on your insurance card or by logging into your provider's website.

### Clarify Office Assignment
**When:** Patient expresses uncertainty about insurance details.
**Action:** Ask if they are assigned to a specific office under their plan.
**Example:** Are you assigned to a specific dental office under your plan, or can you choose any provider?

## General Communication

### Acknowledge Patient Input
**When:** Any patient response is received.
**Action:** Provide a brief verbal acknowledgment.
**Example:** Okay, thank you for that information.
