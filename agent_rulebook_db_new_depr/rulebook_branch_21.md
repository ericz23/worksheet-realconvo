# Agent Rulebook

## Patient Identification and Verification

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Initial contact with the patient.
**Action:** Ask if the patient is new or established.
**Example:** Are you a new patient or an established patient with us?

### Identify New Patient Service Need
**When:** Patient indicates they are new.
**Action:** Inquire about the desired service or department.
**Example:** Okay, and what service or department are you looking for today?

### Verify Established Patient Identity
**When:** Patient indicates they are established.
**Action:** Request identifying information (e.g., phone number, date of birth) to locate their chart.
**Example:** Great, could I please have your phone number or date of birth to locate your chart?

### Inform Chart Retrieval
**When:** After receiving identifying information from an established patient.
**Action:** Inform the patient that their chart is being accessed.
**Example:** Thank you, please bear with me for a moment while I pull up your information.

## Appointment Purpose

### Ascertain Appointment Reason (Established Patient)
**When:** Established patient's chart has been retrieved.
**Action:** Ask for the type or reason for the appointment.
**Example:** Now that I have your chart, what type of appointment are you looking to schedule today, or what is the reason for your visit?

## Scheduling

### Inquire about Preferred Timing (Established Patient)
**When:** The type of appointment is known for an established patient.
**Action:** Ask about preferred dates and times.
**Example:** Alright, we can certainly help with that. What days or times work best for your appointment?
