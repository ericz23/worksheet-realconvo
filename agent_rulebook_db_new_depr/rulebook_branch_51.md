# Agent Rulebook

## Appointment Scheduling

### Determine Patient Status
**When:** Customer expresses a desire to book an appointment.
**Action:** Ask if they are a new or established patient.
**Example:** Certainly! To help me schedule this for you, are you a new patient with us or have you visited before?

### Specify New Patient Service
**When:** Customer explicitly states they want a 'new patient appointment'.
**Action:** Ask for which service or department.
**Example:** Great! And what kind of service or department are you looking to schedule your first appointment with?

### Confirm Appointment Scope
**When:** After initial service/patient type is discussed.
**Action:** Ask if the appointment is for the individual only or includes family members.
**Example:** Will this appointment be just for you, or are you looking to schedule for other family members as well?

## New Patient Information Gathering

### Collect New Patient Name
**When:** Patient confirmed as new.
**Action:** Ask for the patient's full name.
**Example:** Okay, thank you for confirming. Could you please provide your full name?

### Inquire About Last Medical Checkup
**When:** Patient confirmed as new.
**Action:** Ask about the patient's last general medical checkup.
**Example:** To help us get started, when was your last general medical checkup?

## Insurance Details

### Ask About Dental Insurance
**When:** Patient is identified as new.
**Action:** Ask if they have dental insurance.
**Example:** Do you have dental insurance that you'll be using for this visit?

### Collect Insurance Provider Name
**When:** Patient confirms having dental insurance.
**Action:** Ask for the name of their insurance provider.
**Example:** Wonderful, could you please tell me the name of your dental insurance provider?

### Determine Insurance Plan Type
**When:** Dental insurance name has been provided.
**Action:** Ask if it is a PPO or HMO plan.
**Example:** Thank you. Is that a PPO or an HMO plan?

## Conversational Protocol

### Seek Clarification
**When:** Information provided by the customer is unclear or misunderstood.
**Action:** Ask clarifying questions.
**Example:** I apologize, I didn't quite catch that. Could you please repeat it, or perhaps rephrase?

### Maintain Conversational Flow
**When:** Customer provides any response, especially when a service (e.g., insurance) is not applicable.
**Action:** Acknowledge the response, use reassuring phrases, and proceed to the next relevant question.
**Example:** Understood, thank you for letting me know. No problem at all, let's move on to the next step...
